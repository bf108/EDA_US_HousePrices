Investigation of the most important features of a property for predicting sale price.¶

by Ben Farrell¶

Date: 15/05/2020¶

Investigation Overview¶

The aim of this investigation was to produce useful visualisations and insights into features of a property which provide a useful basis upon which to predict Sale Price.

Dataset Overview¶

The data set consists of information regarding 1460 properties sold in Ames Iowa, with 79 explanatory variables describing (almost) every aspect of residence.

The dataset can be found on: Kaggle

Feature documentation available: Ames Housing Data.

Distribution of SalePrice¶

A simple distribution of SalePrice will reveal a right skew which doesn't follow a normal distribution. However, if the SalePrice is transformed using a Logarithmic Transformation, the distribution is much closer to a normal distribution.

Build Quality is a good indicator of SalesPrice¶

In terms of categorical variables, Overall Quality (ordinal) had the strongest correlation with logarithmic SalePrice. Once again this is intuitive, as it is common to pay proportionally more for higher quality.

Vinyl material is associated with Higher Overall Quaity Rating¶

In the lowest quality properties (Less than 5), wood and metal are the most popular building materials.

In the mediocre properties (Between 5-6), wood is by far the most common build material. Then in the higher quality properties (7 and above) vinyl is the most common build material.

Very few properties are rated less than 3 and more than 8, so there is a lack of data in these ranges.

Neighbourhood also plays an import factor in property price¶

The dataset consisted of 25 Neighbourhoods. The price of homes did vary considerably in some neighbourhoods, but there was certainly a correlation between neighbourhood and house price. Some neighbourhoods only contained properties at the lower end of the price scale, such as Meadow Village, whilst others contained a substantial percentage in the upper price band, such as Northridge.
